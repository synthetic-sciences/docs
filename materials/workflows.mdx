Each recipe shows what to type into the agent and what comes out the other side. Adapt them to your system.

## DFT relaxation and band structure

> Relax this halide perovskite structure in VASP at PBE level. After convergence, compute the band structure along the standard high-symmetry path with HSE06.

What happens:

1. The agent reads the structure (CIF or POSCAR).
2. Generates `INCAR` for ionic relaxation with PBE.
3. Sets up `KPOINTS` and `POTCAR` automatically.
4. Runs the relaxation locally or on Modal/Lambda for larger cells.
5. Generates the high-symmetry path with seekpath.
6. Runs the HSE06 band structure calculation.
7. Plots the band structure and reports the band gap.

## ML potential relaxation and dynamics

> Relax this 1024-atom perovskite supercell with MACE-MP-0, then run NPT dynamics at 300 K for 100 ps. Report the lattice parameters and ionic conductivity.

What happens:

1. Loads the structure into ASE.
2. Attaches the MACE-MP-0 calculator.
3. Runs cell and ionic relaxation.
4. Runs NPT dynamics with the Nose-Hoover thermostat.
5. Computes the time-averaged lattice parameters.
6. Identifies migrating ions and computes the diffusion coefficient.

## Phonon calculation

> Compute the phonon dispersion of this hexagonal SiC structure with phonopy. Use a 3x3x2 supercell and DFT forces from VASP. Plot the dispersion and density of states.

What happens:

1. Generates the supercell displacement structures.
2. Runs DFT force calculations on each displaced structure.
3. Constructs the force constants matrix.
4. Computes the phonon dispersion along the high-symmetry path.
5. Computes the phonon DOS.
6. Plots both and reports any imaginary modes.

## Structure prediction

> Predict stable structures of LiCoO2 at 0 GPa using USPEX. Search 50 generations with population size 30, evaluating with PBE+U DFT.

What happens:

1. Sets up USPEX with the requested generations and population.
2. Configures VASP as the energy calculator with PBE+U for Co.
3. Runs the evolutionary search.
4. Tracks the convergence of the energy hull.
5. Returns the lowest-energy structures and how they compare to the known ground state.

## High-throughput screening

> Screen 5000 compositions in the Cu-Sn-S chemical space for thermodynamic stability. Use pymatgen to enumerate, atomate to run DFT, and identify candidates within 50 meV/atom of the convex hull.

What happens:

1. Enumerates compositions with pymatgen.
2. Generates trial structures (using AIRSS or prototype matching).
3. Submits relaxations to atomate (parallelizing on Modal/Lambda).
4. Constructs the convex hull with the Materials Project reference data.
5. Returns the candidates within the requested energy window.

## ML potential training

> Train a NequIP model on this dataset of 50,000 DFT calculations of MOFs. Use a cutoff of 5 A, 4 layers, and 64 features. Validate on a held-out 10% and report force MAE and energy MAE.

What happens:

1. Loads the dataset, partitioning train/validation.
2. Configures the NequIP model with the requested architecture.
3. Trains on GPU through Modal (typically 6-12 hours for this scale).
4. Evaluates on the held-out set.
5. Reports per-element force MAE and total energy MAE.

## Defect formation energy

> Compute the formation energies of Si-vacancies in 4H-SiC across charge states -2 to +2. Use a 4x4x2 supercell at PBE level with finite-size corrections.

What happens:

1. Generates the defect supercells.
2. Runs DFT relaxation for each charge state.
3. Computes formation energies using the chemical potential of Si.
4. Applies finite-size corrections (Freysoldt or Kumagai).
5. Plots the formation energy vs Fermi level diagram and identifies charge transition levels.
