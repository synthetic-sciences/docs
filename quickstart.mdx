You can go from a blank slate to an active research graph in a few minutes. This page walks you through the core Atlas flow: signing in, creating a node, indexing a source, talking to the agent, and optionally pairing it with the Synci CLI.

<Note>
  This is the **Atlas** quickstart (the research workspace). For the [Synsci CLI](/cli/introduction) terminal coding agent, see the [Synsci CLI quickstart](/cli/quickstart).
</Note>

<Steps>
  <Step title="Sign in to Atlas">
    Go to [atlas.syntheticsciences.ai](https://atlas.syntheticsciences.ai) and sign in. Atlas provisions your account automatically on first login, there is no separate registration step.

    After signing in you land on the **Dashboard**, which shows your research graphs and a spotlight search bar.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Create a project node">
    From the Dashboard, click the **New graph** button (or the floating action button on the canvas). Give your root node a title that describes the research topic or question you are starting from.

    Every node in Atlas is either **staged** (editable, work in progress) or **committed** (frozen as durable research history). Start staged, you can commit once findings are stable.

    <Tip>
      Think of a root node as the top of a research tree. You will branch child nodes from it as experiments, source summaries, and insights accumulate below it.
    </Tip>
  </Step>

  <Step title="Add a knowledge source">
    Open the **Sources** panel from the sidebar (or navigate to `/sources`). Paste the URL of a paper, repository, documentation site, or dataset you want Atlas to index.

    Once indexed, the source becomes searchable by the agent, Oracle jobs, and unified search. You can also attach source chunks directly to nodes as evidence.

    <Note>
      Full external source indexing requires the knowledge layer to be active. If you are on a plan that includes knowledge features, indexing starts immediately after you add a URL.
    </Note>
  </Step>

  <Step title="Open the agent and chat">
    Navigate to the **Agent** page (`/agent`) from the sidebar. The agent panel has three tabs:

    - **Canvas**, the graph canvas showing your nodes, edges, and staged work.
    - **Agents**, a live view of running or recent sub-agent instances and their telemetry.
    - **Files**, the project filesystem for artifacts, reports, and sub-agent outputs.

    Type a message to the agent. Unlike a generic chatbot, the Atlas agent hydrates from your actual graph state before responding. It reads your nodes, research log entries, project files, and indexed sources. Ask it to summarize what you have so far, suggest next experiments, or search for relevant prior work.

    <Tip>
      You can open the Research modal from anywhere in the app with **Cmd+Shift+K** (or **Ctrl+Shift+K** on Windows/Linux). It gives you quick access to Ask, Oracle, Tracer, and Document search without leaving your current view.
    </Tip>
  </Step>

  <Step title="Stage your first experiment (optional)">
    Ask the agent to design an experiment for your root hypothesis. The agent will propose an 8-section blueprint:

    1. Hypothesis restatement
    2. Falsification criterion
    3. Method
    4. Expected outcomes
    5. Baseline and control
    6. Metrics
    7. Data configuration
    8. Rigor review

    The proposal appears as a card in the chat. **Approve** it to mint the token required to run compute; **reject** it to log the decision as a dead end and iterate.

    <Warning>
      Approving an experiment proposal authorizes the agent to spawn a compute-spending sub-agent. Review the blueprint carefully before approving, the approval is explicit and logged.
    </Warning>
  </Step>

  <Step title="Pair with Synci CLI (optional)">
    If you want a terminal agent that runs the experiments your Atlas nodes describe, install [Synci CLI](/cli/introduction):

    ```bash
    npx synci
    ```

    Synci CLI signs into the same Synthetic Sciences account, pulls your credentials, and runs scientific workflows in the right [research mode](/cli/research/research) for the job. Atlas keeps the long-running graph; Synci does the work.
  </Step>
</Steps>
