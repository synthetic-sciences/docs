Each recipe shows what to type into the agent and what comes out the other side. Adapt them to your physics setup.

## Quantum circuit simulation

> Build a 50-qubit QAOA circuit for this Max-Cut problem with depth 3. Simulate ideal and noisy execution under a depolarizing channel of 0.001 per gate, and report the optimal cut found.

What happens:

1. The agent encodes the Max-Cut problem as a QUBO.
2. Builds the QAOA ansatz at depth 3 in Qiskit.
3. Runs the ideal simulation and computes the expectation.
4. Adds the noise model and re-simulates.
5. Reports the best cut, the approximation ratio, and the difference between ideal and noisy results.

## Incompressible Navier-Stokes

> Solve the incompressible Navier-Stokes equations on this 2D mesh of a cylinder in cross-flow at Re=200. Integrate for 50 dimensionless time units and measure the drag coefficient and Strouhal number.

What happens:

1. Imports the mesh into FEniCS or OpenFOAM.
2. Sets up the IPCS (incremental pressure correction) scheme.
3. Configures boundary conditions for inflow, outflow, and the cylinder wall.
4. Time-steps the equations, adapting the time step on stability.
5. Computes the drag and lift forces on the cylinder and reports the Strouhal number from lift oscillations.

## N-body simulation

> Integrate the orbits of the four Galilean moons around Jupiter for 1 million years using REBOUND with WHFast. Sample the eccentricities every 100 years and check for resonance lock.

What happens:

1. Sets up the REBOUND simulation with Jupiter and the four moons at current orbital elements.
2. Configures WHFast with a time step appropriate for Io's orbit.
3. Integrates with checkpointing.
4. Samples orbital elements at the requested cadence.
5. Computes resonance angles and reports whether the Laplace resonance is preserved.

## Lattice QCD calculation

> Compute the pion mass on this 32^3 x 64 lattice ensemble using the Wilson action with kappa=0.156. Use 100 gauge configurations and a smeared source.

What happens:

1. Loads the gauge ensemble.
2. Inverts the Dirac operator on each configuration with smeared sources.
3. Computes the pion two-point correlator.
4. Fits the correlator at large times to extract the pion mass.
5. Reports the result with statistical uncertainty.

## Plasma PIC simulation

> Run a 2D PIC simulation of laser wakefield acceleration with a 30 fs pulse at a0=2 in a 1e18 cm^-3 hydrogen plasma. Use WarpX, integrate for 5000 steps, and report the maximum electron energy.

What happens:

1. Sets up WarpX with the requested plasma density and laser parameters.
2. Configures the moving window to follow the laser.
3. Runs the simulation on GPU, ramping the laser smoothly.
4. Diagnoses the electron energy distribution at the end of the run.
5. Reports the cutoff energy and the energy spread of the accelerated bunch.

## Finite element analysis

> Run a 3D linear elastic FEA on this CAD model under a 100 N point load. Use FEniCS with second-order tetrahedral elements. Report the maximum von Mises stress and its location.

What happens:

1. Imports the CAD geometry and meshes it.
2. Sets up the linear elasticity problem with the requested boundary conditions.
3. Solves the system.
4. Computes the von Mises stress field.
5. Reports the maximum stress and the spatial coordinates where it occurs.
